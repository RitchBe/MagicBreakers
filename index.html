WHO WE ARE

" I saw professional people doing professional things at the right place and the right moment. Everybody was happy !" Peter, Spectator

Magic breakers brings breakdance, acrobatics and comedy together in various representations going from street performances to entertainment for corporate meetings or more traditional showcase for venues or private parties. The team is composed of three dancers with their own specialties. The first member is Jeremie: the kangooroo and acrobat of the crew. Then comes Tibs: the seductive, funny and entertaining host of the group. And last but not least, Ritchy: the headspine master who is also a young and charismatic model. These three artists have years of experience as entertainers and have already been traveling all around the globe by means of their art (London, Sydney, Los-Angeles, New-York, Lisbon, and many others). Their acrobatic movements defying the laws of physics, the good vibe that is brought to the audience through their positive energy, and the inspirational message addressed to the kids at the end of the show are some of the main reasons why these modern entertainers are able to build a crowd in a few minutes and perform anywhere in the world.

THE TEAM

Jeremie

Jeremie Houet, alias Acro and also called the kangaroo of the crew due to his passion for Australia, is the jumper of the crew. Born in 1991, he has been doing gymnastic and other extreme sports since he was 8. When traveling to the United States for learning English, Jeremie found the opportunity to perform with a local team of street performers that brought him all the way to Las Vegas for a “Cirque du Soleil” representation. He has been performing on the street or in the more traditional venues since then while studying Economics and Data Analysis.

Thibs

Thibault Mundele also known as Tibs, the oldest of the team, is the host that entertains the crowd with his words and humour. He was born in 1990 and started dancing after a short but intensive career as skater when he was 14. Tibs is almost as comfortable on his hands than on his feet which is why he got the title of Handstand Master in the group. In 2017, he won the title of “Winner of Best Street Performers” in Australia. In the same year, he was invited to give a speech for TeddX Talk in Bruxelles where he explains how traveling opportunities does not depend solely on luck. When he is not traveling for Street Shows, he is away with his partner in crime, Hayley, for their traveling blog.

Ritchy

Richard Houet, known as Ritchy, is the youngest member of the team. Born in 1995 and raised in a family with 3 older brothers already addicted to acrobatic disciplines, Ritchy started practising gymnastic and breaking moves really young. Since then, he has won several breakdancing competition and became the Headspine master of MagicBreakers. He has a geek side too as he also is a freelancer in Web Developpment.
